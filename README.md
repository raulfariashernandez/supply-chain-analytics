# Supply Chain & Inventory Analysis

This repository contains data analysis projects focused on Supply Chain and Inventory Management — combining a real-world dataset from a beauty products company with a simulated warehouse operation to identify inefficiencies, evaluate supplier performance, and support data-driven business decisions.

## Projects

**Notebook 1 - Supply Chain Analysis (Beauty Products)**
Real-world dataset from Kaggle analyzing a beauty products supply chain. Covers revenue performance by product type, critical stock identification, defect rate by product category, shipping cost efficiency, and supplier performance evaluation.

**Notebook 2 - Warehouse Inventory Analysis**
Simulated warehouse dataset with realistic operational structure. Analyzes revenue by product category, stock replenishment risk with priority classification, supplier quality comparison, warehouse efficiency by revenue and on-time delivery rate, and lead time performance by supplier.

## Tools Used
Python · Pandas · Jupyter Notebooks · Google Colab · Kaggle · GitHub

## Key Findings

**Critical Inventory Risk**
5 products identified as critical in the beauty supply chain - SKU34 (Skincare) with only 1 unit in stock against 602 units sold, representing an immediate stock out risk.

**Supplier Performance**
4 out of 5 suppliers maintain an efficient cost ratio below 1% of revenue. Supplier 4 is the only underperformer at 1.31% - nearly double the average - requiring immediate review.

**Warehouse Efficiency Gap**
North Warehouse leads in revenue at $800,730 but records the lowest on-time delivery rate at 61.4% - a critical operational risk for the highest-volume location in the network.

**Lead Time Risk**
Supplier D averages 30.28 days delivery time - more than double compared to other suppliers, posing a consistent supply chain risk for dependent SKUs.
